abstract: Today’s psychologists can choose from many different software packages for
  developing and administering psychological experiments, with the most appropriate
  package typically varying from task to task. For simple experiments based on short
  questionnaires, straightforward tools such as Survey Monkey or Google Forms can
  be most appropriate. For experiments depending on specialist hardware and accurate
  response time measurement, one might instead adopt the Python package PsychoPy (Peirce
  et al., 2019). For experiments administered over the Internet, one could also consider
  the Javascript package jsPsych (De Leeuw, 2015). For experiments involving many
  online participants interacting in complex networks, the Dallinger framework will
  typically be preferable. Many more such tools exist, each specialised for a particular
  aspect of psychological research. psychTestR constitutes a new tool for developing
  and administering psychological experiments. It occupies a special niche within
