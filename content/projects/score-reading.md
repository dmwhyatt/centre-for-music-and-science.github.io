## Musical score reading and notation design

We investigate how musicians read and interpret musical scores, exploring the cognitive and perceptual processes involved in translating notation into performance. This research combines experimental psychology with insights from music theory and pedagogy.

A major focus is the Leverhulme-funded project *Score Design for Music Reading: Cognitive and Artistic Perspectives*, which asks whether standard notation can be redesigned to better support the purposes for which it is used, drawing on evidence from readers’ behaviour and experience.
